A note on daBits:
daBit is a double authenticated bit which refers to a bit doubly secret shared in two fields. It is becoming extremely useful primitive for many applications of MPC:

- https://eprint.iacr.org/2019/207.pdf : daBit(p, 2^k) for p prime is used to construct MPC for mixed circuits (arithmetic and boolean), good example of mixed circuit is for machine learning. In the paper they use SVP (since it requires arithmetic circuit Ax + b and Boolean circuit MAX).

- https://eprint.iacr.org/2019/1300.pdf : daBit(p1,p2) Here daBit is used for SPDZ setup phase - for setup based on homomorphic encryption scheme they use a scheme that require the same bit in two prime fields

- https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/214.pdf daBit(p, 2) using daBit for threshold EdDSA, specifically to convert between sha512 output bits to field elements

- https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/338.pdf dabit(2, p) the newest work (all papers btw are from the last 12 months). They introduce a concept of extended daBit and show how it is helping to speed up common functions such as integer comparison, truncations, shift, and so on. An example is deep learning

My understanding that each iOS device discovered other iOS devices (using bluetooth and wifi) to form a mesh network.

Devices would then encrypt their GPS location using their Apple ID key, then broadcast that to the mesh network to be then uploaded to iCloud after connecting to one device with internet access. No other device could decrypt the message naturally.

The device owner could then locate their own device by decrypting the location information using their Apple ID.
