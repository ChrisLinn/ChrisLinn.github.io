https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/1478.pdf Robust Subgroup Multi-Signatures for Consensus :
- another paper from the GLOW authors (https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/096.pdf )
- authors noticed that existing multisig schemes are not suitable for consensus because there is no identifiable accountability - it is enough for a single party to broadcast a wrong local signature and the entire multi sig will be invalid (in byzantine consensus we assume the existence of faulty signers).
- most cases of previous multisigs were aimed for wallets where this is a non issue
- The main contribution is a tweak to the Boneh et al MSP construction (from Compact Multi-Signatures for Smaller Blockchains). It is actually very smart change - see 4.1: step 1 is to let each signer prove correctness of its local sig, step 2 is to combine only the valid local signatures.
- result is new multisig RSMSP
- Introduction is being rewritten
