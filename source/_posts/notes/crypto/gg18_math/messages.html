Max Zab
After carefull rethinking the whole situation, i realized my mistake. I think my mind is clouded by amount of calculatong, coding and debugging I made lately. Anyways, the answer to my own question is that:

In case of 2-of-2 threshold sig, after Feldman VSS step, each party end up with knowledge of 3 points out of 4. There is still countless amount of possibilities what 4th point, the one that opponent kept secret, could be. So, real secret key is following x = -1*x_1 + 2*x_2 . So we only know what fraction do we contribute to real private key. We know what fraction is contributed by other party, but we still don't know x_2, since 4th point was kept secret from us.

How ever I still think that in case of n-of-n schema, Feldman VSS step is unnecessary. It is only necessary for t-of-n cases, which is more general case.
