Hi @nikkolasg , so the good news is that both the original paper and the ZenGo variant are supposed to not be affected by this attack. The bad news is that in both cases this is done by an additional rounds of communication, by way of first committing to nonce/key commitments. This means that this is possibly not state of the art and there should be a way to implement 2 round eddsa ( based on Musig 2 or FROST maybe) . I didn’t check for MuSig2, however in general I believe bitcoin schnorr uses a different convention from ed25519 regarding the equation to be verified. Besides that there’s the issues of working with curve25519 ( need to make sure you are in the subgroup of prime order along the entire protocol), finally there are some design choices in eddsa ( e.g. the way nonce is generated) that you need to accept as deviation or work hard to support ( see Nigel’s https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/214.pdf )
