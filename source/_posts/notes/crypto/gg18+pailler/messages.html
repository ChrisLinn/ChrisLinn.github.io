A common choice for g in paillier is g = n+1 . Paillier encryption requires to compute g^m mod n^2 which can be simplified to gm +1 mod n^2 (check it with binomial expansion) which is what you see in the code. in multiparty ecdsa gg18 protocol you refer to each party generates a paillier keypair and publish its public key ( n is sufficient) to thee other parties
