Our aim is to improve exactly this computation. Our protocols allow a receiver to determine which ciphertext to decrypt without having the receiver download all the ciphertexts. I think their paper is generic and allow senders to specify a policy. You can think of our work as setting this policy to be "decryptable by the receiver's secret key" which is the protocol specified in section 6.2 of https://eprint.iacr.org/2018/1094.pdf

Now their solution is naive where they dont care about the efficiency of the receiver since that is not the problem the paper wants to solve. We on the other hand want to ensure that the receiver of messages does not need to do constant computation only.

So i wouldnt generalize to say they both solve subsets of the same problem. I would put it as "Private Signaling solves one special case of the matchmaking problem in an efficient way"

Okay, so dialing can be seen as an application of private signaling.
Dialing is a protocol by which pairs of users establish a shared secret. And private signaling is a general protocol by which a sender can convey the location of a message (which may be encrypted) efficiently to a receiver.

Thus, I imagine using private signaling, one could do dialing efficiently by performing some kind of key exchange on the board.
