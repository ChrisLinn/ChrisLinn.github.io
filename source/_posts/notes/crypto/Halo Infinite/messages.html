I should preface this by saying that I'm largely an "accidental" author because a previous paper (https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/081.pdf) was subsumed into the Halo Infinite paper ðŸ˜‚ The better person to dig into the nitty-gritty cryptographic detail is Ben Fisch (@bfisch).

The TLDR for me is that Halo Infinite generalises the Halo ideas so that they work with more polynomial commitment schemes (PCS) beyond Bulletproofs and KZG. It's not too surprising that additive PCS such as DARK and Dory also yield Halo-like proof carrying data (PCD), but the construction nonetheless provides a satisfying unifying framework for all relevant-in-practice PCS. And as @gakonst points out, this includes FRI-based Halo PCD which is quite surprising and very cool.

A possible consequence of FRI-based Halo PCD could be that we do not need relatively new and exotic hash functions (e.g. Rescue, Poseidon) to make STARK recursion practical. Instead SHA256 could do just fine because amount of expensive hashing in the recursive circuit is significantly reduced versus "naive" FRI-based recursion. Another possible consequence may be that Sloth + FRI-based Halo PCD could give us the first practical post-quantum VDF. (Please DM me if you are interested in exploring that.) FRI-based Halo PCD may also be the basis for a post-quantum zkVM for Ethereum 3.0.
