Publicly Verifiable Zero Knowledge from (Collapsing) Blockchains (SVV20 ) (https://eprint.iacr.org/2020/1435.pdf)

- Public verifiable zero knowledge (PVZK) or NIZKs generally require some trusted setup assumptions - like a trusted crs or the random oracle.
- PVZK from a blockchain assumption of chain quality (high level idea, Sec 1.1.2) :
‚Äî assume every block has a distinguished field v (e.g. coinbase address or wallet)
‚Äî let d be a parameter such that for any sequence of d blocks observed - a majority of the blocks will have v generated by honest parties that have used independent randomness (similar to chain quality). The requirement is that v be a high min-entropy value.
‚Äî The pvZK proof (FLS approach) is to prove an OR of two statements - either ‚Äúx in L‚Äù OR ‚ÄúPreviously I have predicted the majority of v values in the last d blocks‚Äù
- Such ideas were also previously presented in https://eprint.iacr.org/2017/935 (GG17) which worked with only the PoS blockchains and a static adversary. SVV20 works for any blockchain and an adaptive adversary.
- Collapsing blockchain basically means that the adversary controls all blockchain players. SVV20 ensures that zero knowledge is guaranteed even if the blockchain collapses.
- This work also presents an attack on GG17 which they call ‚ÄúAttack of the clones‚Äù (Star wars referenceüòÜ) where an adversary by writing a clever smart contract is able to clone the capability of a honest player to perform computations using her secret key. (See Sec 2)
