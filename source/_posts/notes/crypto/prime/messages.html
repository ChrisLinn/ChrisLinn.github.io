The original paper suggests doing rejection sampling until H(N,g,t,y) is prime (find smallest i st H(N,g,t,y,i) is prime). This is slower though, so instead you can hash only once, and look for the primes nearby. The problem is that you have a bias toward primes that are “first”. Like if you have p1 and p2=p1+2, then p1 will have a higher chance of appearing than p2.
