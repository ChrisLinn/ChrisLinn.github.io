Alan Szepieniec
If you have physical access to a piece of hardware that makes ECDSA signatures, you can deploy a range of hardware attacks like observing the power consumption, reducing the voltage, destroying wires or bombarding them with a laser to flip their value ... the goal is to make the device do something it wasn't designed to do, observe the resulting behavior, and break the cryptosystem armed with those observations. This paper suggests another hardware attack or expands on already known but underestimated hardware attacks.

The attack suggested here is essentially exploiting that for a deterministic signature scheme, you use the same 'nonces' when you sign a message twice. However if you get an attack that introduces small errors in the choice of r or k (e.g. w/ a lazer as Alan mentioned), and can make the target repeatedly sign the same message, you can reduce to a biased nonce / related LLL attack. (e.g. https://fc19.ifca.ai/preproceedings/104-preproceedings.pdf, https://toadstyle.org/cryptopals/62.txt)
01:11
There are some tables/experiments later on (2,3,4) that show that this works with a surprisingly large error rate being introduced (Though I'm not sure what type of physical attack would give you such high / targeted corruption rates)
