Our most recent work on threshold DSA is on eprint, although it has been circulating in the community since February. We have two main results: one-round online signing, and identifiable aborts. The latter allows the parties to identify (and "punish") the parties responsible for a DoS attack (which are inevitable in protocols with honest minority). None of the previous proposals achieve that. The one-round online techniques are similar to the ones in the recent paper of Canetti et al (which does not achieve identifiable aborts) -- main difference is that we focus on minimizing computation and identifiable abort while they focus on minimizing rounds and proactive security. Both papers start from our previous work on threshold DSA (CCS'18) and we are excited to see that work spawning so much interesting research.Â
